Further Vectors

You have probably seen and learned vectors from A-Level Maths, and quite briefly in GCSE.

These were just 2D vectors for the x axis and y axis.

In Further Maths, we start to look at vectors in 3D.

As you probably know, a 2d vector comes in the column form:
\[ \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \\ \quad\quad \text{where: } x = \text{x axis unit}, \quad y = \text{y axis unit} \]
Or, 'IJ' form: \[ xi + yj \quad\quad \text{where: } x = \text{x axis unit}, \quad y = \text{y axis unit} \]

Similarly, a 3D vector is in the column form:
\[ \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{pmatrix} \quad\quad \text{where: } x = \text{x axis unit}, \quad y = \text{y axis unit}, \quad z = \text{z axis unit} \]
Or, 'IJK form': \[ xi + yj + zk \quad\quad \text{where: } x = \text{x axis unit}, \quad y = \text{y axis unit}, \quad z = \text{z axis unit} \]

the equation of a straight line in vector form is given as:
\[ r = a + lambda b \]
